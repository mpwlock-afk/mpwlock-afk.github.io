Holographic displays moved out of science fiction and into hotel lobbies, hospital corridors, shop windows and exhibition stands. If you have seen a life-size person or product floating inside a glass cabinet and wondered how it works, this guide explains the technology in plain language and shows where it delivers real value.

## What is a hologram display?

A hologram display is a screen that shows a subject, a person, a product or an animation, as a sharp, three-dimensional image that appears to float in space. The most capable versions use a **transparent LCD** panel inside a sealed cabinet. Bright foreground content sits against a true-black background, so the subject looks like it is really there at human scale.

The effect is strongest when the content is produced for depth. A presenter filmed against black, a 3D-rendered product or a live stream all read as a genuine, life-size presence rather than a flat video on a screen.

## How does a hologram display actually work?

Three things combine to sell the illusion:

1. **A transparent display panel.** Light areas of the image glow; black areas stay invisible, so the subject seems to hang in mid-air.
2. **Depth-aware content.** Footage shot or rendered against black, with careful lighting, gives the eye the cues it needs to read three dimensions.
3. **A controlled cabinet.** Anti-glare safety glass and even internal lighting keep the image crisp in bright, real-world environments.

Add a touch layer and the hologram becomes interactive. The [Holobox](/products/holobox/) uses 20-point infrared touch, so visitors can browse a catalogue, start a consultation or explore a product with their hands.

## Hologram display vs Pepper's Ghost vs LED

People often lump every floating-image trick together. They are not the same:

- **Pepper's Ghost** reflects a flat projection off angled glass. It works, but it is dim, needs a large hidden space and cannot be touched.
- **LED and video walls** are bright and large, but the image is opaque and flat. There is no sense of depth or transparency.
- **Transparent LCD hologram displays** give you a bright, sharp, touch-interactive 3D image in a compact, self-contained cabinet you can place almost anywhere.

For most commercial uses, the transparent LCD approach is the practical winner.
