A hologram display is one of the most reliable ways to stop traffic at an event. A life-size presenter, a floating product or a brand ambassador in 3D draws crowds, gets photographed and gets remembered. Renting lets you create that moment without buying the hardware. Here is how it works.

## Why rent a hologram display

For a one-off event, renting beats buying on almost every measure:

- **No capital outlay.** Pay for the days you need, not the hardware you would store for the rest of the year.
- **The full wow factor.** A rented [Holobox](/products/holobox/) delivers the same life-size, interactive 3D presence as a permanent installation.
- **Scale on demand.** Need a showstopper? Combine units into a [Hologrid](/products/hologrid/) wall for the event, then scale back down.

## What a rental typically includes

Every event is different, but a standard hologram display rental covers:

1. **The display**, sized to your stand or stage, from [The 43](/products/the-43/) to the life-size 86-inch Holobox.
2. **Delivery and installation** at your venue.
3. **Content loading** and, where needed, **live streaming** of a remote presenter through Holocontrol.
4. **On-site or remote support** for the duration.

We scope content production with you in advance, because the content is what turns a screen into a hologram people talk about.

## When to rent and when to buy

- **Rent** for events, launches, trade shows, conferences and short campaigns.
- **Buy** when the display runs continuously, for example a permanent [hospitality concierge](/sectors/hospitality/) or a [retail showroom](/sectors/retail/) display. For the full breakdown, see our [hologram display cost guide](/news/hologram-display-cost/).

A common path: rent for your first event, measure the engagement, then invest in a permanent display once you have seen the impact.
