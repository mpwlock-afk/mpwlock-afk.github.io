"How much does a hologram display cost?" is the first question almost everyone asks, and the honest answer is: it depends on what you need. This guide breaks down exactly what drives the price so you can budget with confidence, whether you are buying a permanent installation or renting for a single event.

## What drives the price of a hologram display

Four factors move the number more than anything else:

1. **Display model and size.** A tabletop [Holobox Mini](/products/holobox-mini/) sits at the entry level. A life-size [86-inch Holobox](/products/holobox/) is a premium installation. [The 43](/products/the-43/) sits in between, and a multi-unit [Hologrid](/products/hologrid/) wall is the largest investment.
2. **Buy vs rent.** A short-term rental for an event costs far less up front than owning a unit outright, but a permanent display is cheaper per day over its lifetime.
3. **Content production.** The hardware is only half the experience. Filming a presenter against black, or producing 3D product content, is a real line item, and it is what makes the hologram look spectacular rather than ordinary.
4. **Management and support.** Remote content updates, live streaming through Holocontrol, installation and ongoing support all factor in for permanent deployments.

## Buying vs renting: a simple rule of thumb

- **Rent** when you need a hologram for a single event, a launch, a trade show or a short campaign. You get the wow factor without the capital outlay. See our [hologram display rental guide](/news/hologram-display-rental/).
- **Buy** when the display will run continuously, a permanent concierge, a retail showroom, a hospital reception, because the cost per day falls dramatically over months and years of use.

Many customers start by renting for an event, see the engagement first-hand, then invest in a permanent installation.

## How to get the best value

The biggest value lever is not the hardware discount, it is matching the right display to the job and investing in content that uses the depth properly. A well-produced hologram on the right-sized display outperforms an oversized unit running a flat video loop every time.
